ACM-W

CSU Fullerton ACM-W supports, celebrates, and advocates for the full engagement, education, and opportunities of women in all aspects of the computing field. We endeavor to provide a wide range of programs and services to students and advance, in the larger community, the contributions of technical women students.

About ACM-W

The goal of ACM-W is to get more girls interested in computer science by exposing them to programming at a young age. ACM-W on the California State University of Fullerton campus has only been around for barely a year. However, the collaboration with the Girl Scouts of Orange County and the funding from Google gives ACM-W the opportunity to accomplish this goal. We are excited for this opportunity to influence young girls about programming and increase the number of girls in computer science.

ACM-W Teaching

The purpose of ACM-W in this event is to work with the Girl Scouts of Orange County and teach girls about the world of engineering. The goal of ACM-W is to get more girls interested in computer science by exposing them to programming at a young age.

During the Spring 2016 semester, the ACM-W Chapter at CSUF and the Girl Scouts of Orange County participated in a mentorship program called Creative Coding. With sponsorship from IgniteCS at Google, they explored Turtle Graphics and Ozobots. Watch us on YouTube
